Whether you allow the tea gardens to function with 15, 25 or 50 percent workforce during this pandemic, our daily wage labourers will be the ones to bear the physical burnt of the decision.
-MBKS Trust
13 April at 21:30
We started our campaign to ameliorate the woes of our daily wage labourers in the tea gardens nearly a fortnight back with the help of Dr. Sudarsan Tamang, Sikkim University. Other benevolent souls joined in following his clarion call.
Tukvar TE, Second Division, lies at the important road joining Darjeeling to Jorethang, Sikkim. Limbu Busty is the epicentre of this tea estate. We distributed essential commodities to hundred families there. We have been supplementing the ration the workers receive from PDS by giving them pulses, gram, white pea, potato, onion, cooking oil, salt and disinfectant soaps, enough to last a family a month.
Under the glaring sun of a hot summer afternoon, they lined up with great discipline and waited for their turn patiently. Old matriarchs formed the majority of the beneficiaries today. The samaj members from the surrounding village were there to help us. As always, we thanked the tea garden workers and asked them to take this help not as a favour on them but as gratitude from the citizens of the Hills for the immense toil and labour they put in to make Darjeeling a brand all over the world.
We have now completed thirteen tea gardens so far around the Hills. There is one common thread that binds the different lives we have touched so far. The fate of our tea garden workers lies in someone else's hand and they do not have control over their own destiny. The decision to allow whatever percentage of workforce back in the tea gardens without any health insurance and safety equipments and without any visible safety norms followed is a slap on their face and is testimony to the fact that they shall always be treated as 'labourers' rather than human resources by the powers that be.
Who shall be held responsible if the pandemic, which has hitherto been under control in the Hills, spreads in the tea gardens due to the lack of social distancing norms in the workplace? Who shall be responsible for even a death in our tea gardens due to this inhumane decision? Will their kin receive any compensation? Will their medical expenses be borne by the management or the government? Does the PM 'cares' about our people and does the CM's 'relief' give respite to the century old economic exploitation of our workers? The world may not remain the same after this pandemic is won over but the world of our workers may never change, it seems!
Happy Nepali New Year to all!
